Hands-On Exercise

Build your pipeline: raw materials → PRD → requirements → journey

Exercise 20 minutes

Work through each step of the pipeline using your chosen AI tool. Use the template or your own materials.

Your three steps:

Step 1: Raw materials → PRD 7 min

Feed your transcripts / feedback into your AI tool. Ask it to produce a structured PRD with problem statement, target user, scope, and success metrics.

Step 2: PRD → Requirements 7 min

Take your PRD and ask AI to extract user stories with acceptance criteria. Validate that each requirement traces to a real user need.

Step 3: Requirements → Journey diagram 6 min

Use your requirements to generate a user journey diagram. Identify the highest-stakes moment in the flow.

Tips for better output:

Be specific: Include real quotes and concrete details

Push back: Challenge anything that feels generic or wrong

Iterate: Don't accept the first output — refine it

Chain outputs: Each step's output feeds the next step

If stuck:

Start with: "Here is a customer call transcript. Extract the top 3 pain points and the core problem the customer is trying to solve."

Module 4: Build Sprint

Build & Deploy Your MVP

Build Sprint 15 minutes

Turn your PRD into a deployed product

Steps:

  1. Open v0.dev or Bolt.new

    Paste your requirements as the prompt

  2. Iterate once

    Refine based on output

  3. Deploy

    Get your live URL

Deployment options:

  • v0: Click Deploy → Vercel
  • Bolt: Already hosted, click Share
  • Replit: Click Run → copy URL

Keep it simple:

  • Use localStorage (no backend needed)
  • Focus on your #1 user story first
  • Working > perfect
  • You can iterate after launch

If stuck:

Paste your requirements + code into Claude and ask:

"Help me implement [specific user story]. Here's my current code..."

  • Export to your outreach tool

Why PMs should use Clay:

  • Talk to users before building more
  • Validate demand with real outreach

Quick Clay vocabulary:

Table

Your list of leads (like a spreadsheet)

Enrichment

Adding data to each row (company size, email, etc.)

Formula

AI-powered columns that generate content

Integration

Connections to LinkedIn, Apollo, etc.

Module 5: GTM Sprint

Step 1: Define Your ICP

GTM Sprint 4 minutes

Your design work already defined your user — now get specific

ICP Template:

Title/Role: [e.g., Head of Product, Founder] Company Type: [e.g., B2B SaaS, Series A-B] Company Size: [e.g., 20-100 employees] Industry: [e.g., DevTools, FinTech] Signal: [e.g., recently raised, hiring PMs]

Example ICP:

For a feedback tool MVP:

  • Title: Founder, Head of Product
  • Company: B2B SaaS startups
  • Size: 5-50 employees
  • Industry: DevTools, Productivity
  • Signal: Active on Twitter/LinkedIn, recently launched
Module 5: GTM Sprint

Step 2: Build Your Lead List in Clay

GTM Sprint 8 minutes

Create a table with 10-15 qualified leads

  1. Create a new table in Clay

    Name it after your product/ICP

  2. Add people using "Find People"

    Use LinkedIn search or import from a list. Filter by your ICP criteria.

  3. Enrich with company data

    Add columns for company size, industry, recent news

  4. Find email addresses

    Use Clay's email finder enrichment

Goal: 10-15 people who match your ICP with valid emails

Module 5: GTM Sprint

Step 3: Generate Personalized Outreach

GTM Sprint 5 minutes

Use Clay's AI to write personalized messages for each lead

Add an AI column:

  1. Click "Add Column" → "AI"
  2. Choose "Generate Text"
  3. Write your prompt template
  4. Reference other columns with {{}}
  5. Run for all rows

Outreach prompt template:

Write a short, friendly cold email to {{First Name}}. Context: - They are {{Title}} at {{Company}} - Company does: {{Company Description}} - I built: [your MVP description] - My MVP URL: [your deployed URL] The email should: - Be under 100 words - Reference something specific about them - Ask if they'd try the product - No salesy language
